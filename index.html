01 — About

I like problems that don't have a clean dataset yet.

My work moves between molecular biology, wearable accessibility tech, and conversational AI — usually wherever the data is messy and the impact is direct. I care more about whether a system can explain itself than whether it hits a benchmark.

  • HEALTH Applying AI to drug discovery pathways and building explainable healthcare assistants that show their reasoning, not just their output.
  • ACCESS Designing offline, on-device systems — like navigation aids for visually impaired users — that work without depending on a network connection.
  • SIGNAL Crossing over into hardware and signal processing, using deep learning to classify real-world radio signals captured with SDR hardware.
  • PRODUCT Shipping full applications — multi-agent simulators, chatbots, and deployed web frontends — end to end, not just notebooks.

Most recently, I've been building MediGuide AI, an explainable healthcare assistant, and refining an AI-driven pipeline for analyzing non-ribosomal peptide synthetase pathways used in antibiotic and anti-cancer drug development.

I write code the way NRPS builds peptides: one well-defined module joined to the next, assembled in order, until the whole thing does something none of the parts could do alone.
